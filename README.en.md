# tablature.io

A free tool to write and share drum rhythms in a simple, visual way — right in your browser. No installation, no sign-up.

---

## Who is this for?

For drummers, percussionists, music teachers, and anyone who wants to **write down a rhythm** without needing to read sheet music.

If you've ever scribbled a groove on a piece of paper, tablature.io does the same thing — cleanly, neatly, and easy to share.

---

## What is a drum tablature?

It's the most practical way to write drum music. Instead of traditional sheet music (which takes years to learn to read), a tab uses a simple grid:

- **Horizontal lines** — each line represents a piece of the drum kit (kick, snare, hi-hat, etc.)
- **Symbols on the line** — show when that piece should be played

Every drummer knows this format. It's the same one you see in forums, YouTube tutorials, and music books.

---

## How it works

### Pick the instrument you want to add

The instrument panel shows all available drum pieces — from the kick drum to the cymbals. Click one to select it.

### Click the grid to place a note

Once an instrument is selected, click anywhere on the grid where it should sound. The note snaps automatically to the nearest beat, so you don't have to worry about alignment.

### Adjust anything

- Drag a note to move it to a different beat or instrument
- Click a note to delete it
- Press **Ctrl+Z** to undo any mistake

### Add as many bars as you need

Got a groove longer than one bar? Just click **"+ Bar"** to extend the tab.

### Hear it play back

Hit **Play** to listen to your tablature. Each piece plays its corresponding sound, making it easy to check if the rhythm sounds the way you imagined.

### Export and share

When you're done, download your tab as a **PDF** to print or send, or as an **image** to post anywhere.

---

## Nothing gets lost

Everything is saved automatically. Close the browser, come back later — your tab will be right where you left it.
